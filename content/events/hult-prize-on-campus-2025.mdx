## What happened

On February 21, 2025, AIS hosted the annual **Hult Prize On-Campus Hackathon** — the local stage of the world's largest student social entrepreneurship competition. **Seventeen CMU-Africa students** formed **seven teams** to build impact-driven ventures aligned with the year's Hult theme.

### Top three teams

1. **CheckMe** — AI-powered breast cancer detection *(1st place)*
2. **Moonsquare Limited** — gold-backed stablecoin for African B2B trade *(2nd place; later 2nd place nationally)*
3. **Umuganda Energy Finance** — clean energy financing mechanisms *(3rd place)*

### National results

**Three teams advanced to the national level**, with Moonsquare Limited finishing **2nd in the national Hult Prize round** — confirming CMU-Africa's reputation for high-quality, mission-driven solutions.

### Skills built

The competition pushed students through the full early-stage venture stack: **problem framing, impact modelling, business model design**, and live pitching.
