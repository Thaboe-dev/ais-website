## What happened

The 2025 edition of the **Tartans Innovate Hackathon** ran from **October 14–21** as a 6-day development sprint, with the final pitch event on **October 25, 2025**. The hackathon was led by CMU-Africa's **Industry Innovation Lab (IIL)** with student mobilisation and support from AIS.

### The numbers

- **70+ participants**
- **25 team submissions** — each requiring at least 10 user interviews, a digital prototype, and a written solution abstract
- **48-hour community vote** to select the top 10
- **10 finalist teams** at the live pitch event with panel Q&A

### Winning teams

1. **ECHO** — real-time voice translation app supporting English, Kinyarwanda, and Swahili *(1st place)*
2. **Milli-Lib (MiLi)** — points-based micro-library of 1–2 minute explainer videos for peer-to-peer learning *(2nd place)*
3. **Tartan OppHub** — AI-powered opportunity hub for scholarships and internships *(3rd place)*
4. **Kurunzi** — academic support platform syncing Canvas assignments and study groups *(4th place)*
5. **Campuslink** — integrated AI platform for academic planning and event management *(5th place)*

### Prizes

International conference sponsorships and vouchers — designed to keep winning teams in the rooms where their ventures can grow.

### What's next

The post-event report calls for **structured incubation support, mentorship, and follow-up workshops** for the winning teams — work AIS will help coordinate alongside IIL.
