## What happened

From **March 10–12, 2026**, AIS partnered with the **Inclusive Fintech Forum™** — one of Africa's premier fintech conferences, held annually in Kigali and supported by the **Kigali International Financial Centre**, the **National Bank of Rwanda**, and the **Global Financial Technology Network (GFTN)**.

### The partnership

Through this community partnership, AIS secured:

- **60 complimentary delegate vouchers** for CMU-Africa students
- Each voucher valued at **$200** — totalling **$12,000** in access delivered to the community

### AIS on stage

Our Club President was invited for a fireside chat at the Forum — one of the region's flagship annual fintech gatherings.

**Stephen David**, AIS President, joined the fireside chat **"Competence as Currency: Cultivating Tech Leadership and Career Pathways in Inclusive Fintech,"** moderated by **Robert "Bob" Okello**, Founder of **Maarifasasa Limited**.

The panel discussed the projects and student-led startups being built within the AIS ecosystem at CMU-Africa — amplifying the club's visibility on an international platform and connecting our members with practitioners shaping inclusive fintech across the continent.

### Why this milestone matters

This partnership demonstrates AIS's growing capacity to **secure external institutional relationships that deliver direct, measurable value to CMU-Africa students** — exactly the kind of compounding outcome a student-led organisation can build over multiple executive teams.
