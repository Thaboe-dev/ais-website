## What happened

On **14 February 2026**, AIS ran the **Future Founders Pitch Competition** at **Carnegie Mellon University Africa** — a one-day pitch bootcamp for high school students that culminated in a live pitch competition.

The day was designed to give young learners **an early, structured introduction to entrepreneurship**: how to spot a problem worth solving, shape it into an idea, and communicate it clearly under time pressure.

### How the day ran

The day combined **hands-on mentorship, group work, and a final pitch showdown**:

- **Morning bootcamp** — AIS members and CMU-Africa graduate students facilitated small breakout groups, walking each team through problem discovery, customer thinking, and a simple business-model canvas
- **Team work** — Students worked in cross-school teams on real problems they see in their communities, from education to health to civic tech
- **Pitch competition** — In the afternoon, each team took the stage to pitch their idea to a panel, defending it under Q&A
- **Awards ceremony** — Winning teams walked away with certificates, CMU-Africa merchandise, and, most importantly, the confidence that comes from having pitched in front of a real room

### Impact

Africa's next founders are still in high school today. Bringing them onto a university campus, into a room built for entrepreneurship, and letting them stand up and pitch their own ideas is one of the most direct ways AIS can invest in the long-term pipeline of African innovation.

It's also a reminder that entrepreneurship isn't something you switch on after graduation — it's a way of seeing the world that we can help train much earlier.
