## What happened

On February 20, 2026, AIS co-hosted a session with the **CMU-Africa Energy and Embedded Systems Club** featuring **Alp Tilev** — President, CTO, and Co-founder of **Ampersand** — Rwanda's leading electric motorcycle and battery-swap company. The theme: **"Scaling Across Borders in Africa."**

### Why Ampersand

Ampersand is one of East Africa's most important clean-energy startups — having pioneered electric motorcycles and battery-swap infrastructure across Rwanda and Kenya, and a leading example of capital-intensive climate technology built for African market conditions.

### What was covered

- The full arc from **prototype to scale** — and what changed at each stage
- Navigating **infrastructure gaps**, **regulatory environments**, and **consumer behaviour change** in different African markets
- Lessons specific to **hardware entrepreneurship** that don't show up in software-first founder talks
- A practical **blueprint for cross-border expansion** in clean-energy technology

### Cross-club collaboration

The co-hosting with the Energy & Embedded Systems Club reflects AIS's commitment to interdisciplinary collaboration — pairing entrepreneurship-first conversations with the deep-technical communities on campus.
