## What happened

On November 7, 2023, AIS — then operating as the **Student Entrepreneurship Club** — officially launched at Carnegie Mellon University Africa. The launch event drew **80 students** from CMU-Africa, the African Leadership University, Davis College, and the University of Rwanda.

### The conversation

Students engaged directly with industry leaders from:

- **Jasiri** — pan-African talent investor backing first-time founders
- **250 Startups** — Rwanda's flagship early-stage accelerator
- **Eric Rutayisire**, founder of **CHARIS UAS** — a Rwandan drone company building Africa-grown technology

The speakers shared candid lessons on building innovative, market-relevant solutions in an emerging market — and what it really takes to move from idea to launch.

### Impact

The launch was the first signal that CMU-Africa students wanted a space where entrepreneurship was practical, not theoretical. It set the tone for everything AIS would build next: bootcamps, hackathons, delegations, and eventually a full Leadership Summit two years later.
