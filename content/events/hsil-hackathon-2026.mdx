## What happened

AIS was thrilled to partner with the **Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health (Health Systems Innovation Lab — Rwanda Hub)** and **Carnegie Mellon University Africa** for an intensive, high-impact weekend of innovation.

The **HSIL Hackathon 2026** — run globally by HHSIL through various hubs — was all about **leveraging Artificial Intelligence to solve critical challenges in healthcare**. AIS hosted the Kigali edition, bringing together the brightest minds to design tech-enabled solutions that can transform health systems across the continent.

### Event details

- **Dates:** 3rd & 4th April, 2026
- **Venue:** Carnegie Mellon University Africa
- **Location:** Bldg Plot No A8, Kigali Special Economic Zone, Regional ICT Center of Excellence
- **Theme:** *Building High-Value Health Systems: Leveraging Artificial Intelligence*

### Why this hackathon mattered

- **Innovate:** Apply AI to real-world health system problems
- **Impact:** Build the future of resilient, accessible, and high-value healthcare in Africa
- **Connect:** Work alongside faculty, clinicians, and industry mentors under one roof

### The winners

**1st Place — Team #VitaLabs 🏆**
Team 7 (VitaLabs) emerged 1st place winners at the Kigali hub of the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health Hackathon 2026. Their project, **ImaraCare Systems**, stood out for its brilliant use of AI to build higher-value, more resilient health systems in Africa.

Champions:
- Hafiz Adjei
- Paul Mugume
- Martin Koome
- Innocent Anyaele
*(all Carnegie Mellon University Africa)*

**2nd Place — Team 4: Code for Cure (CFC)**
Their project showcased incredible technical depth and a deep commitment to solving real-world healthcare gaps. These Women in Tech proved that precision and purpose are the keys to a resilient Africa.

Brilliance behind CFC:
- Grace Kaimburi
- Ukachi Eze-Mbey
- Nimat Sadiq

**3rd Place — Team 9: MultiScan AI**
Rounding out the top three, MultiScan AI demonstrated the power of **multimodal AI in transforming healthcare accessibility** — a massive win for innovation and collaborative spirit.

### Why this one mattered

Hosting the Rwanda hub of a global Harvard hackathon was a milestone moment for AIS: it put CMU-Africa on the map as a serious venue for healthtech innovation, and gave our members a chance to build alongside — and be judged by — leaders from Harvard, CMU-Africa, and the broader African health ecosystem.
